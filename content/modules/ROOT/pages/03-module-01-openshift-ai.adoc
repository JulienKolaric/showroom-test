= Module 1: OpenShift AI in Action
:source-highlighter: rouge
:toc: macro
:toclevels: 2

This module shows how NovaTech goes from "we should do something with AI" to a live inference endpoint on OpenShift {ocp_version}.

== Part 1 — Why AI Belongs on OpenShift

=== Know

_Customer challenge: AI experiments live everywhere except the platform the company already pays for and knows how to operate._

**Business Impact:**

* Data science buys tools. App teams buy other tools. Security sees neither until production.
* GPU capacity sits idle in one silo while another team queues for access.
* Every pilot reinvents deployment, routing, and auth.

**Value Proposition:**

OpenShift AI gives NovaTech a familiar home for models: same RBAC, same routes, same GitOps habits, plus dashboards purpose-built for ML workflows.

According to link:https://www.redhat.com/en/technologies/cloud-computing/openshift/openshift-ai[Red Hat OpenShift AI^], teams can develop, deploy, and monitor AI models on the same foundation used for cloud-native applications.

=== Show

**What I say**:
"NovaTech does not need a parallel universe for AI. They need a lane on the highway they already built. That lane is OpenShift AI."

**What I do**:

. Open the **OpenShift AI** tab in the right panel
. Log in with `{user}` / `{password}` if prompted
. Orient the audience to the main areas: projects, workbenches, serving

**What they should notice**:

* A dashboard that looks like part of OpenShift, not a random third-party portal
* Project-scoped resources, same mental model as `Namespace` in OCP
* A path from experimentation to serving without leaving the platform

**If asked**:

Q: "Is this a separate product?"::
A: "OpenShift AI extends the platform you already operate. Same cluster, same operators, same support story."

== Part 2 — Projects and the Developer Experience

=== Know

_Developers do not want a ticket to get a notebook. They want a button._

**Current vs Desired State:**

* Now: "File a request, wait for a VM, install packages, hope it matches prod."
* Goal: Self-service project, curated runtimes, push to serving when ready.

**Stakeholder Impact:**

* **VP Engineering** cares about speed to market
* **Platform team** cares about quotas, RBAC, and not becoming the ML help desk
* **Security** cares about where models and data live

=== Show

**Optional visual**: Simple diagram of Dev → OpenShift AI project → Serving route

**What I say**:
"This is the moment NovaTech's lead developer stops DMing the platform team and starts actually building."

**What I do**:

. In the OpenShift AI dashboard, open an existing data science project (or the demo project prepared for this environment)
. Point out workbenches and serving runtimes available in the project
. Switch briefly to the **OCP Console** tab and show the same project namespace

**What they should notice**:

* The project maps cleanly to a Kubernetes namespace
* Resource limits and RBAC still apply (guardrails, not chaos)
* Developers get autonomy without root on the cluster

**Business value callout**:
"Self-service here does not mean ungoverned. It means governed self-service, which is the only kind that scales."

== Part 3 — Live Inference (The Fun Part)

=== Know

_The demo wins when the audience sees text stream back from a model, not when you explain transformers._

**Why this matters:**

* Stakeholders believe what they can type into
* A working endpoint turns "AI strategy" into "AI shipping this quarter"
* Serving on OpenShift reuses routes, TLS, and monitoring patterns teams already know

**Reference needed**: Exact model name and latency metrics for your prepared environment should be validated against your cluster deployment.

=== Show

**What I say**:
"Alright, less slide-deck, more magic trick. Let's talk to a model running on OpenShift."

**What I do**:

. In OpenShift AI, navigate to **Deployments** or **Serving** (wording may vary by OpenShift AI version)
. Open the prepared InferenceService or model deployment for this demo
. Show status **Ready** (or narrate startup if still warming)
. Copy the inference route or use the integrated test UI
. Send a short prompt, for example: "Explain why running AI on OpenShift is a good idea in two sentences."
. Show the response streaming or returning in the UI

**Optional CLI moment** (Terminal tab):

[source,bash]
----
curl -k "https://{inference_route}/v1/chat/completions" \
  -H "Content-Type: application/json" \
  -d '{
    "model": "{model_name}",
    "messages": [{"role": "user", "content": "Say hello from OpenShift AI!"}],
    "max_tokens": 64
  }'
----

**What they should notice**:

* A real HTTP endpoint, not a localhost script
* Authentication and routing handled like any other OpenShift service
* The same cluster that runs their apps can run their models

**If asked**:

Q: "Which models are supported?"::
A: "Serving runtimes support popular open models. Your team pins versions and sources per environment, same as container images."

Q: "What about cost?"::
A: "You scale nodes and replicas like any workload. Start small, measure utilization, grow with demand."

== Part 4 — From Pilot to Production

=== Know

_Pilots fail when they cannot graduate. OpenShift is the graduation path._

**Business Value:**

* One platform team, one set of playbooks
* GitOps-friendly manifests for InferenceServices and routes
* Observability hooks align with existing SRE practices

**Competitive angle (light touch):**

DIY GPU clusters are exciting until someone asks who patches them at 3 a.m. OpenShift AI is the boring-in-a-good-way answer.

=== Show

**What I say**:
"The pilot is not a throwaway. The manifests you use in the lab are cousins of what you promote to prod."

**What I do**:

. Briefly show a deployment manifest or the OpenShift AI view of the InferenceService configuration (no secrets on screen)
. Mention namespace isolation, routes, and resource requests as production-ready knobs
. Return to the three key messages: **IA avec OpenShift**, speed with guardrails, same cluster and skills

**What they should notice**:

* Continuity from demo to operational model
* No second platform to hire specialists for
